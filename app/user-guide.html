1. What Is This App?

NGL Accounting Suite is a desktop tool built for logistics accounting. It has three main tools:

📄
Merge Tool

Combine invoices, PODs, and bills of lading into organized PDF packets — one per container.

📧
Invoice Sender

Upload a CSV of invoices, match them to PDF files, and send them through QuickBooks Online automatically.

👥
Customer Manager

Keep track of customer emails, document requirements, and send preferences.

🔒
Your files stay on your computer. All PDF merging and Excel processing happens locally. Nothing is uploaded to the internet.

4. Merge Tool — Combining Documents

The Merge Tool combines your invoices, PODs (Proof of Delivery), and bills of lading into neat PDF packets. It has two modes:

Auto Mode (most common)

Upload an Excel file and PDF files. The app reads the Excel, finds which PDFs belong to which containers, and merges them automatically.

Manual Mode

Just drag in 2 or more PDFs, reorder them, and merge. No Excel needed — great for quick one-off merges.

  1. Upload your Excel file — Drag the .xlsx file into the left drop zone. The app reads it and finds columns like "Container Number" and "Invoice Number" automatically.
  2. Upload your PDFs — Drag all your PDF documents (invoices, PODs, bills of lading) into the right drop zone. You can drop them all at once.
  3. Choose a merge option — "Per Container" is the default and most common. It creates one PDF per container with all matching documents inside.
  4. Click "Run Auto Merge" — The app matches each PDF to its container using the Excel data, then combines them. A progress bar shows the status.
  5. Download your results — When it finishes, you'll see the merged files listed. Click "Download All" to get a ZIP file, or download individual files.
📊
Excel Column Matching: The app is smart about column names — "Container #", "CNTR NO", "Container Number" all work. Same for invoice columns.

Manual Mode (Quick Merge)

For quick one-off merges without Excel data:

  1. Switch to Manual Mode using the toggle at the top of the page.
  2. Drag in 2 or more PDFs.
  3. Reorder them by dragging — the order you set is the order they'll appear in the final PDF.
  4. Click "Merge" to combine and download.

AI Agent Panel

On the right side of the Merge Tool, you may see the AI Agent Panel. This connects to QuickBooks Online (QBO) and TMS to automatically fetch missing documents.

💡
Auto-Fetch: If the agent is connected and logged into QBO/TMS, click "Auto-Fetch Missing" to have the app automatically download any PODs or invoices you're missing.

  1. Upload your CSV — This should be your invoice export file with columns like Invoice Number, Customer Name, and Amount.
  2. Review the table — The app matches each invoice to a customer in your database and pulls their email address. Any row with a missing email shows No Email — you'll need to add that customer's email in the Customer Manager first.
  3. Try Test Mode first — Check the "Test Mode" box to do a dry run. Everything runs normally, but no emails are actually sent. Great for verifying everything looks right.

⚠️
QBO must be connected. The Invoice Sender needs the agent to be logged into QuickBooks Online. If the QBO badge shows "Not Connected," go to Settings and log in first.

8. Troubleshooting & Tips

Common Issues

ProblemSolution
"Agent Offline" badge The background service isn't running. Close and reopen the app. If using the non-desktop version, run Start Agent.bat.
QBO shows "Not Connected" Go to Settings and click "Re-login." A browser window will open for you to sign in to QuickBooks.
Excel columns not recognized Make sure your spreadsheet has columns for Container Number and Invoice Number. The app is flexible with naming (e.g., "CNTR #" or "Container No." both work).
PDFs not matching to containers Check that the PDF file names contain the container number or invoice number. The app uses the file name to match.
Invoice shows "No Email" That customer doesn't have an email on file. Go to Customers, find them, and add their email address.
Send failed for an invoice Usually means the QBO session timed out. Try re-logging into QBO in Settings, then retry the failed invoices.

Tips & Best Practices

💡
  • Always test first — Use "Test Mode" in the Invoice Sender before doing a real send.
  • Keep customer emails updated — Invoices can only be sent if the customer has an email on file.
  • Use "Per Container" merge — It's the most organized format and what most customers expect.
  • Check the Failure Report — After a merge, look at the failure report to see if any PDFs couldn't be matched.
  • Export your customer list — Periodically export from the Customer Manager as a backup.
  • Save to Folder — Use "Save to Folder" instead of "Download All" when working with large batches.
